List of the best books I'd recomend everyone to read

  • "Meditations" by Marcus Aurelius:

    A timeless collection of personal writings by the Roman Emperor, offering profound insights on Stoic philosophy, self-reflection, and living a virtuous life.

  • "The Count of Monte Cristo" by Alexandre Dumas:

    A gripping tale of betrayal, revenge, and redemption, following Edmond Dantès' journey as he transforms from a wronged young man to the enigmatic Count of Monte Cristo.

  • "Three Comrades" by Erich Maria Remarque

    Set in post-World War I Germany, this novel delves into the deep bonds of friendship among three war veterans, capturing the struggles, hopes, and resilience in the aftermath of war.

  • "How to Win Friends and Influence People" by Dale Carnegie:

    A classic self-help book that offers timeless advice on interpersonal skills, communication, and building meaningful relationships for personal and professional success.

  • "Financier" by Theodore Dreiser:

    The first book in the Trilogy of Desire, it follows the rise of ruthless businessman Frank Cowperwood in late 19th-century America, exploring themes of ambition and morality.

  • "The Twelve Chairs" by Ilya Ilf and Evgeny Petrov:

    A satirical adventure-comedy set in post-revolutionary Soviet Russia, where two conmen hunt for hidden diamonds in a series of humorous escapades.

  • "The Little Golden Calf" by Ilya Ilf and Evgeny Petrov:

    A satirical sequel to "The Twelve Chairs," following the charismatic conman Ostap Bender on his quest to find a legendary golden calf statue.

  • "The Red and the Black" by Stendhal:

    Set in 19th-century France, this novel follows Julien Sorel, a young man of humble origins, as he navigates society's complexities, ambition, and passion. A classic tale of love, politics, and social stratification, it offers a deep exploration of human nature and the pursuit of success.

  • "Crime and Punishment" by Fyodor Dostoevsky:

    A masterpiece of psychological fiction, the novel revolves around the tormented mind of Raskolnikov, a poverty-stricken student who commits a heinous crime and grapples with the psychological consequences and moral dilemmas. Dostoevsky delves into the depths of human consciousness, guilt, and redemption in this thought-provoking work.

  • "The Godfather" by Mario Puzo:

    A gripping crime novel that delves into the world of the Italian-American mafia, centered around the Corleone family. The story explores themes of power, family loyalty, and the dark underbelly of organized crime. With its unforgettable characters and complex plot, "The Godfather" remains a classic in the crime fiction genre.
